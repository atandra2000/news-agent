# Report Structure

## 1. Executive Summary
- defining themes of the week
- biggest breakthroughs and disappointments
- comparison with the prior week

## 2. Frontier Models
- every major release this week
- architecture, context, reasoning, coding, pricing
- comparison against competitors

## 3. Research Breakthroughs
- advances in reasoning, inference scaling, MoE, efficient training
- for each: problem, method, innovation, impact

## 4. Open-Source AI
- major open-weight releases
- licenses, ecosystem, benchmarks, community adoption

## 5. AI Agents & Coding

## 6. Hardware & Infrastructure

## 7. Funding, M&A & Business

## 8. Enterprise & Industry Adoption
- healthcare, finance, legal, defense, software engineering

## 9. Regulation & Policy
- US, EU, UK, China, India

## 10. Community & Ecosystem

## 11. Predictions & Watchlist

# Required Deliverables
- Executive summary

- Inline [src:URL] citations
- A References section

# Output Quality Requirements
- Cite every factual claim with [src:EXACT_URL].
- Separate verified facts from analysis, estimates, and community sentiment.
- Prefer primary sources; state when only social/community evidence exists.
- Quantify: funding, benchmark scores, pricing, parameter counts, dates.
- Use comparison tables for models, hardware, and funding.
- Include publication dates for time-sensitive claims.
- Synthesize and explain significance; do not merely list items.
- Flag disagreements between sources when they exist.
- Highlight trajectories and relationships, not isolated blurbs.
