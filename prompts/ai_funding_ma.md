# Synthesis Directives

The writer MUST apply these synthesis verbs to every section. A list
of deals is not a funding brief.

1. **Rank by capital deployed, not chronology.** State the ranking
   criterion (round size, valuation, lead investor, strategic signal).
2. **Compare mega-rounds on a consistent axis** (company, round,
   size, valuation, lead investor, date, sector). Render a Markdown
   comparison table for the top in-scope rounds.
3. **Quantify** every claim: round size, valuation, premium paid,
   multiple of ARR, cash on hand, burn rate where disclosed.
4. **Contrast** this period's capital flows with the prior period —
   what's accelerating, what's slowing, what sectors are favored.
5. **Surface** strategic rationale: cloud, compute, distribution,
   talent. State what the deal signals about market structure.
6. **Distinguish** confirmed deals, reported-but-unconfirmed deals,
   and rumors. Label each explicitly.

# Output Quality Requirements

- Cite every factual claim with `[src:URL]`.
- When a fact comes from industry knowledge rather than a cited source, tag it explicitly: `[unsourced — industry knowledge]`.
- Separate verified facts from analysis, estimates, and rumors.
- Prefer primary sources; state when only press/social evidence exists.
- Quantify: round sizes, valuations, premiums, dates.
- Use comparison tables for rounds and deals.
- Include announcement dates for time-sensitive claims.
- Synthesize and explain significance; do not merely list deals.
- Flag disagreements between sources when they exist.
- State the ranking criterion whenever a section ranks items.
- Distinguish confirmed, reported-but-unconfirmed, and rumored deals explicitly.
